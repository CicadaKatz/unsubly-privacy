Unsubly Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 19, 2024

Introduction

Unsubly is a Chrome extension designed to help you manage and unsubscribe from email subscriptions in your Gmail inbox. This privacy policy explains how we handle your data and protect your privacy.

Data Collection and Usage

Unsubly collects and processes the following data:

  • Email Content: The extension scans your Gmail messages to identify subscription emails. This is done through Google's official Gmail API.
  • User Activity: We track which subscriptions you select for unsubscribing to provide the unsubscribe functionality.
  • Local Storage: Subscription information is stored locally in your browser using Chrome's storage API.

Data Protection

We take your privacy seriously:

  • All data processing happens locally in your browser
  • No data is transmitted to external servers except for Google's official APIs
  • We do not collect or store any personal information beyond what's necessary for the extension's functionality
  • We do not share any data with third parties

Permissions

Unsubly requires the following permissions:

  • Gmail Access: To scan and identify subscription emails
  • Storage: To save your subscription preferences locally
  • Tabs: To open unsubscribe links when you choose to unsubscribe

Your Rights

You have full control over your data:

  • You can revoke Gmail access at any time through your Google Account settings
  • You can clear all local data by removing the extension
  • You can choose which subscriptions to manage or unsubscribe from

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new privacy policy on this page and updating the "Last updated" date.
